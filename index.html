Collecting Books

I’ve been getting into collecting books in the last two years, especially the African Writers Series. I am slowly building my collection. There’s just something about having the physical copies, and I like knowing the background behind the books too, not just the stories but what they represent. It kind of feels like I’m creating my own little library over time.

Books I've read this year

  • A Broken People’s Playlist by Chimeka Garricks
  • The Hairdresser of Harare by Tendai Huchu
  • We Are All Birds of Uganda by Hafsa Zayyan
  • Ogadinma by Ukamaka Olisakwe
  • The Secret Lives of Baba Segi's Wives by Lola Shoneyin

Matcha Moments

Another thing I’ve been enjoying is matcha. I didn’t expect to like it this much, but now I’ve been trying to find the best one. I’ve started paying more attention to where it’s from, how it tastes, and the different qualities between them. Some are smoother, some are more bitter, and it’s interesting figuring out what I actually prefer. It’s kind of become a small search, trying different types and seeing which ones stand out. It’s not anything deep, but it’s one of those small things that just makes the day feel a bit nicer.

Learning

I’ve finished learning the basics of Java, and looking back, I feel really good about how far I’ve come. It wasn’t always easy, but getting through it has made me more confident in my ability to learn new things. Now I’ve started learning SQL, and I’m actually feeling quite positive about it. It feels like a natural next step, and I’m enjoying seeing how everything connects. I’m still early in the process, but I feel more motivated and optimistic this time around, like I know I can figure things out if I stay consistent.
